Purpose Of This Page

I'm experimenting with building a website manually (no bootstrap). I was inspired by Bulbapaedia's website, which is really pretty and aesthetic. It's not as fluid as modern websites, but it makes good use of background colours to present a page that is easy on the eyes.

Breakdown Of Model Website

Bulbapaedia appears to have stayed relatively 'stagnant', unchanged from its early days. I believe that I first chanced upon this gem from 2000 to 2010, where moving elements had not yet become the norm. The overlying theme of this webpage is green and there's a generous use of rounded boxes to contain information. By today's standards, many portions of this website are information-dense, which is commendable, especially keeping in mind its purpose as an encyclopaedia. However, its extensive use of images can be rather inconvenient for those with low-speed internet. After all, one of the greatest boons of traditional websites is their high loading speed.

Now, with such high information density, one must use alternative ways to break up text into concise prose while allowing users to distinguish for themselves which they'd rather read. So if I hear someone calling the bullet-points AI-written, my forehead will soon bear an imprint of my palm.

Pros

  1. Straightforward, linear navigation. There are visible (already expanded) trees and nested navigation links, which provides a clear structure of information.
  2. Information-dense. All relevant information is presented directly, without being abbreviated and shortened into soundbites.
  3. Lack of design-friction. Most of the data you could ever want to access is available in one or two clicks rather than being buried in obscure menus.
  4. Lack of dancing elements. I may be in the minority, but it is disorientating and confusing when elements and words flip around, spin or even fade into nothingness when scrolling through a website. This is a mild example. It took me five seconds to load.

Cons

  1. Extensive use of images. Granted, this is an encyclopaedia, so images may be necessary for a comprehensive and informative guide on the topic at hand. As such, I don't see this as a true downside. Some have reported that there are too many advertisements, but I can't comment on that as I use an adblocker.
